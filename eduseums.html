Eduseums

Before making my transition to UI Design, I was (and still am) an educator. My wife is an educator herself, specifically in the arts. As the COVID-19 pandemic started in full and the world went into lockdown, I saw the struggles that educators had in deeply engaging their students in an online setting. It was here that the concept for Eduseums was born. What if there was an app that would allow students to virtually tour museums around the world, engaging in a deep way with artists, individual art pieces and art movements? Students would be able dive headfirst into the world of art and guide their own learning. At the same time, educators would have a stellar tool to teach art in a post-COVID world.

The Process

Research and User Flows

The task for this CareerFoundry assignment was to create an open-ended app for iOS and Android. Using my background in education, I decided to develop Eduseums. I then researched several other app competitors including Smartify, Google Arts and Culture, and apps designed by specific museums, like the Louvre. Based on this research I developed several key features that my app concept would need to have, including features that were important in competitor's apps as well as features that were missing. Utilizing these essential features I created a master user flow that identified how users would navigate through the Eduseums.

Wireframing

After completing the user flow for Eduseums, the process of wireframing ​began.​ This process started with hand drawn low-fidelity wireframes, then mid-fidelity, and finally high-fidelity wireframes. These wireframes were created for both iOS and Android per the CareerFoundry task, along with gesture outlines for both. As you can see in the wireframes, the design evolved and changed through each part of the process.

Design

The origins of the color,font and other design choices for E​duseums​ began with considering what would be welcoming to students of all ages. This presented a challenge to creating something that would be relatable to both a five year old and an eighteen year old. I ended up going with a palette that was youthful and inviting. There was also the challenge of designing for iOS and Android, with each platform optimized.

User Testing

User testing for E​duseums ​was focused on a prototype of the high-fidelity wireframes (link above). Based on this testing, certain design choices were altered and pages adjusted to be more readable/make more sense in general.

Conclusion

Eduseums​ was something I felt very passionate about as an educator and I was happy with the final product. I feel this app could be easily used in classrooms and would be a valuable tool for educators. This is especially true for those in the arts, a subject that is often underserved.
